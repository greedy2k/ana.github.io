My blogs

My blog post this month is How I evaluate College Computer Science Teaching. I get a lot of opportunities to read teaching statements and other parts of an academic's teaching record. I tend to devalue quantitative student evaluations of teaching -- they're biased, and students don't know what serves them best. What I most value are reports of the methods teachers use when they teach. Teachers who seek out and use the best available methods are most likely the best teachers. That is what i look for when I have to review College CS teaching records.

On twitter, people are most concerned with my comments about office hours. Computer Science homework assignments should not be written expecting or requiring everyone in the class to come to office hours in order to complete the assignments. That's an instructional design problem. If there are questions that are coming up oftne in office hours, then the teacher should fix the assignments, or add to lecture, or make announcements with the clarification. Guided instruction beats discovery learning, and inquiry learning is improved with instruction. There is no advantage to having everyone in the class discover that they need a certain piece of information or question answered.
